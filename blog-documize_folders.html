Why doesn't Documize has folders

Because no one can find anything anymore

Thousands of emails and files with hundreds of layered folder structures created on a whim. Duplication of content, lost content, multiple versions flying around...

We thought search would save us from this nightmare, but we were wrong.
The process is broken by design as folders are alphabetically ordered and how we organize content is usually subjective to each individual, this is an ever growing issue in organizations and by design we have addressed it by removing folders altogether.

So how does Documize organize thousands of files?

We allow you two layers of organization Spaces and Categories, with files belonging exclusively to a space and being able to belong to several categories.
The decision of what goes in a space and in a category is actually a pretty big deal, but you can reorganize as you get used to it pretty quickly, our best practices dictate that:

  • First, we divide everything in our system in to (at most) ten large buckets of stuff. We call those our Spaces
  • Then, within each Space, we break things up again in to (at most) ten more buckets. We call them Categories. Everything related to a particular category is in that bucket
  • Finally, we assign each individual document in our system tags to help on organization and visualization
  • Choose simple names for your Spaces, Categories and Tags
  • If a space has too many categories is probably too broad and you want to split it up
  • There should not be any files without Categories, instead find the right home for that document. It must relate to something! Find what category that something is, and if it doesn't exist, create it
  • One file can belong to multiple categories at the same time
