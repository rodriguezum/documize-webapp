Evolution of Documents

Paper

The word "paper" is etymologically derived from papyrus, Ancient Greek for the Cyperus papyrus plant. Papyrus is a thick, paper-like material produced from the pith of the Cyperus papyrus plant which was used in ancient Egypt and other Mediterranean societies for writing.

Paper has thought to be around since 100BC give or take a century and has been an enormous catalyst for information knowledge transfer

The rise of Internet and Digital Documents

Around 1960's IBM Selectric typewriter (a letter-quality printer) created the first word processing program but the growth in usage came in the 1980's when Microsoft released the Microsoft Word processor
Ever since that time clients would create, edit and share their documents however:

  • documents could only be stored locally, once lost... they were lost
  • only one person could edit the documents at a time
  • all the documents could only be viewed on a single device
  • version control was managed by new file names or minimal functionality

Online Document Management

Then came the online editing in early 2000's and the number of tools just exploded, from project management to wiki management tools started springing all over the internet.
People now can collaborate, edit the same document at the same time, view it on multiple devices, have automated translation and so much much
